# Positioning reference

> Canonical reference for `competitive-intelligence` skills.
> Owner: Competitive Intelligence Lead.

This file is the source of truth for *how we describe ourselves* in competitive contexts. Skills like `competitive-analysis` load this file before they generate output, so a competitive brief written by Claude reflects current positioning, not whatever the model guesses.

## Three differentiators we claim

1. **Differentiator one** — short headline. One sentence on what it is and why it matters to the buyer. Avoid jargon.
2. **Differentiator two** — short headline. Same shape.
3. **Differentiator three** — short headline. Same shape.

These are the differentiators every competitive brief should explicitly position against. If a brief omits them, it's missing the point.

## Who we are not for

The customers we deliberately don't serve, and why. Naming non-fit is part of positioning — buyers trust sellers who know who they aren't for.

- *Audience we aren't for #1*, because *reason*.
- *Audience we aren't for #2*, because *reason*.

## Words we don't use

Phrases that have leaked into competitive briefs in the past and shouldn't appear:

- "best-in-class" — generic; replace with a concrete claim
- "industry-leading" — same problem
- "synergies" — corporate filler
- "AI-powered" *unless* the specific AI capability is stated immediately after

## How to use this file

A competitive brief produced by `competitive-analysis`:

- Names the competitor.
- Names *each* of the three differentiators above explicitly and contrasts them.
- Avoids the words listed under "Words we don't use."
- Cites this file's owner if a reviewer disputes positioning claims.

If the buyer is in a segment listed under "Who we are not for," surface that fact rather than trying to position around it.
